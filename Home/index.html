M A R I N E   L I F E

Marine life is known as the plants, animals, and other organisms that live in the salt water of the ocean or in the brackish waters of coastal estuaries. Basically, marine life helps to determine the nature of our planet. Most of the oxygen we breathe is produced by marine life. The coastline is semi-arid and protected by marine life, and some marine life even helps to create new land.

As we know Most organisms first evolved in marine habitats. The oceans provide about 99 percent of the Earth's living space.The first vertebrates appeared in the form of fish and lived only in water. Some of these evolved into amphibians and spend part of their lives in water and part on land.

Other fish evolved into land mammals and later came into the ocean as seals, dolphins or whales. Plants such as kelp and algae grow in water and form the basis for some underwater ecosystems. Plankton and especially phytoplankton are the major primary manufacturers of the general foundation of the seafood chain.
